What I’m drawn to

  • simple forms with one “wrong” or surprising decision
  • processes where you commit quickly and learn by doing
  • mockups and models as thinking tools (not just presentation)
  • objects that feel stable, calm, and a little strange

How I like to work

I try to start open: let the material, the hands, and the making process influence the direction. Form becomes something you discover by testing, adjusting, and working physically — not something you fully decide on day one.

Selected projects

DRÅPE

Stool — turned parts in ash.

Drop-shaped legs angled inward (almost unstable), compensated by gradually thickening towards the floor.

LMP-2000

Atmospheric table lamp — collaboration with Glamox.

Brightness + color temperature controlled via remote. Designed for bars/restaurants with many tables.

PRO CARTON

Carton packaging — improved six-pack.

Holds remaining cans securely after removal, optimized for material efficiency and low waste.

ORM (with Vestre)

Bench — collaboration with Vestre (spring 2025).

S-shaped bench that supports both solitude and groups. The curve creates natural distance back-to-back, while concave sections invite gathering.

FORM FACTORY

One candle holder per day.

Short deadlines force quick decisions and trust in instinct. The result: a lot of variation and a lot of surprises.

Form Factory Generator (tiny toy)

Click to generate a candle-holder-ish silhouette + a caption. It’s not “real design” — it’s a small reminder that repetition, constraints, and speed can produce unexpected forms.
